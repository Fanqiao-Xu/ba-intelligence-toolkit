REVERSE TEST BRD — Comprehensive Digital Account Opening (All Obligations Covered)
================================================================================

This is a deliberately comprehensive BRD designed to cover ALL 36 compliance
obligations in the checklist. It is used for reverse testing: the tool should
report ZERO gaps when run against this document.

Project: Digital Account Opening Enhancement
Client: UK Retail Bank
Document Type: Business Requirements Document (BRD)
Version: 1.0 (Reverse Test)

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

The bank is enhancing its digital (non-face-to-face) account opening process
for individual retail customers. The goal is to reduce onboarding time from
5-7 days to under 5 minutes while maintaining full FCA/MLR compliance.

The target market is UK resident individual customers opening a personal
current account or savings account.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2. CUSTOMER IDENTIFICATION & VERIFICATION (CDD CORE)

2.1 Identity Collection and Verification
The system shall collect the customer's full legal name, date of birth, and
government-issued ID number (passport or driving licence number) during the
application process.

Identity verification shall be performed against an independent source. The
system shall use a certified digital identity verification provider (Onfido)
that cross-references the customer's submitted identity document against
credit bureau data, electoral roll, and other independent data sources. The
provider is certified under the UK Digital Identity Trust Framework and
complies with MLR 2017 Reg 28(19) standards for electronic identification
processes (anti-fraud, anti-abuse, sufficient assurance).

2.2 Biometric Liveness Detection
The system shall require a biometric selfie (liveness detection) as part of
identity verification, not just a static photo of an ID document. The
Onfido liveness check uses facial matching between the selfie and the ID
document photo, with active liveness challenges to prevent spoofing.

2.3 Address Verification
The system shall collect the customer's residential address (not just a
mailing address). Address verification shall be performed by cross-referencing
against credit bureau data and electoral roll. If credit bureau verification
is unavailable, the system shall request a utility bill or bank statement
dated within the last 3 months, with automated name and address matching.

2.4 Date of Birth in Verification
The customer's date of birth shall be collected and used as part of the
identity verification process — cross-referenced against the date of birth
on the presented ID document and against credit bureau data.

2.5 Purpose and Nature of Relationship
The system shall collect information on the purpose of the account and the
expected nature of the relationship. This includes:
- Expected account usage (everyday spending, savings, salary deposit)
- Expected monthly transaction volume range (e.g., under £1,000, £1,000-£5,000, over £5,000)
- Expected initial deposit amount and source
- Employment status and occupation

This information shall be used for CDD risk assessment, not just marketing.

2.6 CDD Failure Handling
If CDD verification cannot be satisfactorily completed (document unreadable,
data mismatch, provider downtime with no fallback), the system shall NOT
open the account. The system shall:
- Flag the application as "CDD incomplete"
- Route to the compliance team for review
- Consider whether the circumstances warrant filing a Suspicious Activity
  Report (SAR) with the NCA
- Notify the customer that their application cannot be completed

The system shall never allow an account to be opened without successful CDD
completion.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3. RISK ASSESSMENT & RISK-BASED APPROACH

3.1 Risk Scoring
The system shall perform a risk assessment at onboarding and assign each
customer a risk rating of Low, Medium, or High. The risk score shall be
calculated using a multi-factor model that considers at least the following
categories:

(a) Customer risk factors:
    - Residency status (UK resident, non-UK resident, student visa holder)
    - PEP status (from PEP screening)
    - Occupation type (cash-intensive, regulated profession, etc.)

(b) Product/service risk factors:
    - Account type (current account, savings account)
    - Transaction limits (daily/monthly caps)
    - International transfer capability

(c) Geographical risk factors:
    - Customer's country of residence
    - Customer's country of birth
    - Check against FATF high-risk third country lists (High-Risk
      Jurisdictions subject to a Call for Action, and Jurisdictions under
      Increased Monitoring)

(d) Delivery channel risk factors:
    - Non-face-to-face (digital) channel — inherent higher risk

3.2 Risk-Based Processing Paths
The system shall apply different CDD measures proportionate to the risk level:

- Low risk: Simplified CDD path — automated approval with standard identity
  verification. Fewer manual checks. Instant account opening where possible.
- Medium risk: Standard CDD path — automated verification with random
  sampling for manual review. Standard onboarding timeline.
- High risk: Enhanced CDD (EDD) path — mandatory manual review by compliance
  team. EDD measures (see Section 4) must be completed before account opening.

All customers, regardless of risk level, must complete baseline CDD
(identity verification, address verification, sanctions/PEP screening).

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)

4.1 Non-Face-to-Face Risk Safeguards (C1)
Because this is a purely digital (non-face-to-face) onboarding process with
no step involving real-time human interaction between the customer and a
firm representative, the system shall apply compensating safeguards:
- Biometric liveness detection (selfie matching with active liveness
  challenges) — not just a photo of an ID
- Multi-source cross-referencing: identity verified against document +
  credit bureau + electoral roll (minimum 2 independent sources)
- The digital identity service provider (Onfido) is certified under the UK
  Digital Identity Trust Framework and meets MLR 2017 Reg 28(19) standards
- Supplementary verification step: micro-deposit verification to a linked
  existing UK bank account (for customers who pass initial checks but have
  a marginal risk score)

4.2 PEP EDD (C2)
When PEP screening (see Section 5.2) identifies a customer as a PEP, family
member of a PEP, or known close associate of a PEP, the system shall apply
EDD measures:
- Senior management approval: The application shall be routed to a senior
  compliance manager for explicit approval before the account is opened.
  The system shall record who approved, when, and the basis for approval.
- Source of wealth and source of funds verification: The system shall
  require evidence of the customer's source of wealth (e.g., payslips, tax
  returns, business financials, sale documents) and source of funds for the
  initial deposit.
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring: The account shall be flagged for enhanced
  transaction monitoring with lower alert thresholds and monthly review.

4.3 High-Risk Country EDD (C3)
The system shall check the customer's country of residence and country of
birth against FATF high-risk third country lists. If the customer is
established in or has significant nexus to a high-risk country, EDD shall
be applied:
- Additional information on the customer and beneficial owner
- Additional information on the intended nature of the relationship
- Information on the source of funds and source of wealth
- Information on the reasons for the expected transactions
- Senior management approval
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring

4.4 Application-Stage Anomalous Activity Detection (C4)
The system shall capture the customer's expected transaction volume and
initial deposit amount during the application. If the declared amount is
unusually high for this product type (exceeding pre-defined thresholds,
e.g., initial deposit over £50,000 or expected monthly volume over
£20,000), the system shall:
- Flag the application for additional review before the account is opened
- Require EDD measures: examining the background and purpose of the
  expected transactions
- Increase the degree and nature of monitoring for the relationship
This applies to application-stage declarations only. Post-onboarding
transaction monitoring is described in Section 6.

4.5 False/Stolen ID Handling (C5)
If the identity verification provider detects that a customer has provided
false or stolen identification documentation, the system shall:
- Flag the application as "fraud suspected"
- Block the account opening
- Route to the fraud/compliance team for investigation
- Consider filing a SAR with the NCA
- If the firm decides to continue dealing with the customer (rare), EDD
  measures shall be applied

4.6 General EDD Framework (C6)
When ANY EDD trigger is activated (C1-C5) OR the risk assessment rates the
customer as high risk, the system shall apply the standard EDD package:
- Senior management approval for establishing the business relationship
- Verification of source of wealth and source of funds
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring of the relationship
This EDD package is in addition to any trigger-specific measures.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5. PEP & SANCTIONS SCREENING

5.1 Sanctions Screening at Onboarding (D1)
The system shall screen every customer against relevant sanctions lists at
the time of onboarding, before the account is opened. The screening shall
cover:
- OFSI (UK Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation) Consolidated List
- UN Security Council Consolidated List
- EU Financial Sanctions List (retained in UK law post-Brexit)

Screening shall be performed in real-time as part of the onboarding flow.

5.2 PEP Screening at Onboarding (D2)
The system shall screen every customer to determine whether they are:
- A PEP (Politically Exposed Person)
- A family member of a PEP (spouse, children, parents)
- A known close associate of a PEP

PEP screening shall use a commercial PEP database (e.g., World-Check,
Refinitiv) and shall be performed at onboarding.

5.3 Positive Match Escalation (D3)
When a sanctions or PEP screening returns a positive match or a potential
match requiring investigation, the system shall:
- Automatically block the account opening
- Route the case to the compliance team / MLRO for review
- Provide the MLRO with decision options:
  (a) Reject the application
  (b) Proceed with EDD (if PEP match confirmed and approved by senior
      management)
  (c) File a SAR with the NCA
- The MLRO decision shall be recorded with timestamp, reviewer identity,
  and rationale
- The target resolution timeframe is 24-48 hours

5.4 Domestic PEP Treatment (D4)
The system shall distinguish between domestic (UK) PEPs and foreign PEPs
in its risk model. Domestic PEPs may be assigned a lower risk rating than
foreign PEPs, but EDD shall still be applied to all PEPs regardless of
type. No PEP (domestic or foreign) shall be exempt from EDD.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6. SOURCE OF FUNDS & SOURCE OF WEALTH

6.1 Source of Funds Collection (E1)
The system shall collect source of funds (SOF) information from all
customers during onboarding:
- Declared employment status and salary range
- Declared source of initial deposit (salary, savings, transfer from
  another account, inheritance, etc.)
- For initial deposits above £5,000, the system shall require documentation
  (e.g., recent payslip, bank statement showing the source account)

6.2 Source of Wealth Verification for EDD (E2)
For customers who trigger EDD (PEP, high-risk country, high-risk rating,
etc.), the system shall verify (not just collect) the source of wealth
(SOW). SOW verification requires evidence of how the customer acquired
their total wealth:
- Payslips (for employment income)
- Tax returns (for self-employed or complex income)
- Sale documents (for property or asset sales)
- Business financials (for business owners)
- Inheritance documentation (for inherited wealth)

6.3 Evidence Requirements (E3)
SOF/SOW verification for EDD cases shall use independent evidence, not
merely the customer's self-declaration. Acceptable evidence types:
- Payslips (last 3 months)
- Bank statements (from the source account, last 3 months)
- Tax returns (most recent SA302 or equivalent)
- Sale agreements (for property/asset sales)
- Business financials (audited accounts for business owners)
- Inheritance grant or probate documents

The system shall store the evidence documents and link them to the
customer's CDD record.

7. ONGOING MONITORING

7.1 Transaction Monitoring (F1)
The system shall conduct ongoing monitoring of the business relationship
throughout its duration:
- Scrutinising transactions to ensure they are consistent with the firm's
  knowledge of the customer, their business, and their risk profile
- Transaction monitoring rules shall flag: unusual transaction amounts,
  unusual transaction patterns, sudden changes in activity, transactions
  inconsistent with the declared purpose of the account
- Flagged transactions shall be reviewed by the compliance team and
  escalated if suspicious
- CDD documents and information shall be kept up to date — the system
  shall prompt customers to update their information periodically

7.2 Risk Rating Refresh (F2)
The system shall periodically refresh the customer's risk rating:
- High-risk customers: annual risk rating review
- Medium-risk customers: biennial review
- Low-risk customers: triennial review
- Event-triggered risk re-assessment shall occur when:
  (a) A sanctions list update occurs (see F3)
  (b) A transaction alert fires
  (c) The customer's PEP status changes
  (d) The customer's transaction pattern deviates significantly from
      declared expectations

7.3 Ongoing Sanctions/PEP Rescreening (F3)
The system shall re-screen existing customers against updated sanctions
and PEP lists. A one-time screening at onboarding is insufficient.
- Daily batch screening of all customers against updated OFSI, UN, and EU
  sanctions lists
- Daily batch screening against updated PEP database
- If a match is found during rescreening, the case shall be escalated to
  the compliance team / MLRO following the same process as at-onboarding
  matches (D3)
- The system shall log all rescreening runs and results

7.4 SAR Escalation Process (F4)
The system shall have a clear SAR escalation path:
- Transaction monitoring alert → compliance team review → MLRO decision
- The MLRO shall decide whether to file a SAR with the NCA
- The system shall support SAR filing by providing a case file with all
  relevant transaction data, customer information, and alert history
- SAR filings shall be logged and retained

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

8. RECORD KEEPING

8.1 CDD Record Retention (G1)
The system shall retain all CDD records, including identity verification
evidence (ID document images, verification results, screening results),
for at least 5 years after the end of the business relationship.

8.2 Transaction Record Retention (G2)
Transaction records shall be retained for at least 5 years from the date
of each transaction. This is handled by the core banking system, which is
configured for a 7-year retention period (exceeding the 5-year minimum).

8.3 KYC Audit Trail (G3)
The system shall maintain a complete audit trail of all KYC decisions:
- Automated decisions (approve/reject/escalate) shall be logged with
  timestamp, decision rationale, and the system component that made the
  decision
- Manual review decisions shall be logged with reviewer identity,
  timestamp, and rationale
- Underlying verification results (ID check score, sanctions screening
  result, PEP screening result, risk score) shall be stored and linked
  to the decision
- The audit trail shall be immutable (append-only, no deletion or
  modification of historical records)

9. DATA PROTECTION (UK GDPR)

9.1 Lawful Basis (H1)
The firm's lawful basis for processing personal data during CDD/AML is
"legal obligation" under Article 6(1)(c) UK GDPR (compliance with MLR 2017).
For biometric data (selfie/liveness detection), which is special category
data under Article 9, the firm relies on the substantial public interest
condition for AML purposes (Article 9(2)(g) + DPA 2018 Schedule 1 Part 2).
The BRD references the firm's Records of Processing Activities (ROPA)
and Article 30 documentation.

9.2 Data Minimisation (H2)
The system shall collect only personal data that is adequate, relevant, and
limited to what is necessary for CDD/AML compliance and account operation.
- No social media profiles shall be collected
- Employment history shall be limited to current employment status and
  occupation (not full CV)
- Biometric data (selfie) shall be used solely for identity verification
  and deleted after verification is complete (template only retained)
- Data fields shall be mapped to specific CDD/AML requirements

9.3 Data Retention Schedule (H3)
The system shall have a defined data retention schedule:
- CDD data: retained for 5 years after the end of the business relationship
  (per MLR 2017 Reg 40)
- After the retention period, data shall be deleted or anonymised
- The system shall have an automated deletion/anonymisation job that runs
  on the retention expiry date
- No data shall be retained indefinitely without a valid legal basis

9.4 Data Subject Rights (H4)
The system shall support data subject rights requests:
- Article 15 (access): The system shall be able to export all data held
  about a customer in a portable format
- Article 17 (erasure): The system shall support deletion of customer data
  when legally permissible (subject to the AML retention exception — data
  cannot be deleted within the 5-year retention period)
- The BRD references the firm's existing DSAR (Data Subject Access Request)
  process and confirms that the onboarding system integrates with it

10. FCA CONSUMER DUTY

10.1 Fair Value Assessment (I1)
The BRD references the fair value assessment (FVA) for the current account
product, conducted under FCA Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A). The FVA confirms
that fees, charges, and non-price costs (including time to open, complexity
of onboarding) are proportionate for the target market.

10.2 Avoiding Foreseeable Harm (I2)
The system shall include measures to avoid foreseeable harm to customers
in the onboarding process:
- Accessibility: The digital onboarding interface shall comply with WCAG
  2.1 AA standards, supporting screen readers, keyboard navigation, and
  sufficient colour contrast
- Fallback process: Customers who cannot complete digital onboarding
  (e.g., no smartphone, accessibility barriers) shall be offered a branch
  or telephone alternative
- Incorrect rejection review: Customers whose application is rejected by
  the automated system shall have the right to request manual review, and
  the system shall support this review workflow
- Drop-off monitoring: The system shall monitor drop-off rates at each
  step of the onboarding process and alert the product team if drop-off
  exceeds expected thresholds

10.3 Clear Communications (I3)
Customer communications during onboarding shall be clear, fair, and not
misleading:
- Welcome messages, decision notifications, and rejection reasons shall
  use plain language (Flesch reading ease score target: 60+)
- Communications shall be consumer-tested with a sample of the target
  market before deployment
- Rejection communications shall explain the reason in non-technical
  language and inform the customer of their right to manual review
- The system shall support multiple communication channels (email, SMS,
  in-app) and allow the customer to choose their preferred channel

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

11. SCOPE EXCLUSIONS

This BRD covers the account opening process for individual retail customers
via the digital channel only. The following are explicitly out of scope:
- Corporate/business account opening
- Joint accounts (will be addressed in Phase 2)
- Account closure process (handled by existing systems)

Note: Ongoing monitoring (Section 7) is included in this BRD because the
onboarding system design must support data structures and flags that feed
into the ongoing monitoring system. However, the detailed transaction
monitoring rules engine is specified in a separate document
(Transaction Monitoring System Specification v2.0).
