================================================================================
AS-IS PROCESS: Current Account Opening (Manual KYC)
================================================================================

PROCESS OVERVIEW:
The current account opening process for individual retail customers relies
heavily on manual review. Customers can start the process in-branch or via
the mobile app, but identity verification and KYC checks are performed
manually by the KYC team.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS:

Step 1: Customer visits branch or downloads the mobile app.

Step 2: Customer fills in the application form (paper form in branch,
        digital form in the app). Information collected:
        - Full legal name
        - Date of birth
        - Residential address
        - Employment status and employer name
        - Expected account usage (salary, savings, etc.)
        - Source of initial deposit

Step 3: Customer uploads ID documents (passport or driving licence) via
        the app, or provides them in person at the branch.

Step 4: Branch staff or onboarding team manually review the uploaded
        documents. This includes:
        - Checking document authenticity (visual inspection)
        - Matching the photo on the document to the customer
          (in-branch only; not possible for app submissions)
        - Checking the name and date of birth match the application form

Step 5: KYC team performs manual checks (2-3 days). This includes:
        - Credit reference check via Experian (automated API call)
        - PEP and sanctions screening (manual query of screening tool)
        - Adverse media check (manual web search for high-profile cases)

Step 6: Compliance team review for cases flagged by the KYC team
        (1-2 days). This applies to:
        - PEP matches
        - Sanctions matches (rare — usually result in rejection)
        - High-value initial deposits
        - Unusual application patterns

Step 7: Account creation in the core banking system (manual entry by
        back-office team).

Step 8: Debit card issuance (5-7 days via post).

Step 9: Welcome pack sent via post.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PAIN POINTS:
- Total time: 5-7 working days
- 40% drop-off at the document upload stage
- High manual effort (3 FTE in KYC team)
- Inconsistent decision-making (different staff apply different standards)
- Poor audit trail (decisions logged in emails and spreadsheets, not
  in a structured system)
- No real-time feedback to customer on application status
- Branch staff spend significant time on document review that could
  be automated

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CURRENT RISK CONTROLS:
- PEP screening: performed at onboarding only, not refreshed
- Sanctions screening: performed at onboarding only, not refreshed
- Transaction monitoring: separate system in compliance team
  (post-onboarding, not part of this process)
- Risk rating: implicit (KYC team uses judgement, no formal scoring)
- Record retention: CDD documents filed in document management system,
  retained for 5 years per AML policy
